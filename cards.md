# The Builder Trail — Card Drafts

Twenty net-new scenes for the four-attribute model: **Money, Health, Relationships, Agency**. Complementary to the events already in `events.js` (no overlap).

**Point of view:** A corporate tech employee trying to become AI-builder-native. They're learning to ship with AI, building real side projects, and gaining optionality, all while keeping their body, family, and friendships intact. Every scene should feel lived-in.

**Design rules (deliberate):**
- **No dominant strategy.** Every choice has at least one cost and one gain. No all-upside options.
- **Agency is not a synonym for "the cool indie choice."** It rises from learning deeply, shipping real things, talking to users, and good judgment. It *falls* when you consume hype, blindly trust agents, build the wrong thing, perform building instead of doing it, or get pulled into reactive busywork. Several scenes make the "agentic-looking" choice the one that *lowers* Agency.
- **Money has real spikes** (`++`/`--`), not just drift. Vests, raises, rent, and runway move it.
- **Attribute counts vary.** Some scenes trade off 2 attributes, some 3, some all 4.

**Notation:** The *Shown hint* is what the player sees before choosing (affected attributes only, no direction). *Hidden impact* is design intent: `+` small, `++` strong, `-` small, `--` strong. *Result copy* narrates the consequence so the player can infer what moved.

**Illustrations:** Crisp, clean 16-bit pixel art (bright and readable, not soft/painterly), one unique image per scene. Cohesion comes from the art style and a shared warm palette with a rust/clay accent (#D97757), NOT from a uniform mood. Each scene uses the light its moment calls for (bright daylight, warm afternoon, golden hour, or night), so the set spans a full range of lighting and settings. Lead with people, places, and objects; show a glowing screen only when the scene is truly about one. First-person framing where it fits. (The per-scene notes below still describe the earlier darker drafts; they'll be refreshed once the regenerated art is locked.)

**Sections:** I. The Day Job · II. Family & Friends · III. Body & Burnout · IV. Craft & Agency · V. Big Bets & Money, plus **Endings** at the bottom.

# Endings

Five endings, two good and three bad. You **win** by shipping **5 project credits** within 26 weeks while all four attributes stay above 0. You **lose** the moment any attribute hits 0, or when week 26 arrives without enough credits. Each ending is keyed to *how* the run resolved, so the player understands what happened.

**Engine mapping** (in `game.js → getEnding()`): Health → 0 = Burnout · Relationships → 0 = Alone with the Repo · Agency → 0, time-out without 5 credits, or Money → 0 = Still a Spectator · shipped 5 with everything healthy = The Balanced Builder · shipped 5 with any meter near empty = Barely Made It. (If you'd rather give Money → 0 its own "Runway Crisis" screen, that's an easy sixth.)

## ★ The Balanced Builder — *win (good)*

> Twenty-six weeks, and five things that didn't exist before now do. I'm still here too. Paid, rested, still married, still curious. I never became a founder or made headlines, but I learned to build for real, and I kept the life that made building worth it. The rarest ending on the trail.

**Illustration:** My own hands easing a laptop closed at golden hour. Through the doorway my family moves in warm light, a "shipped" tab still glowing softly on the screen. Everything lit, nothing broken. *(reuse: ending-builder)*

## ☆ Barely Made It — *win (bittersweet)*

> I crossed the line on fumes. Five credits shipped, and some part of me running on empty: The sleep, the savings, or the people I kept saying "soon" to. It counts. It's a win. I'm going to fix what I let slide, starting tonight, before the bill comes due.

**Illustration:** My POV at the finish. A "5 / 5 SHIPPED" badge blazing, but at the edge of my vision one HUD meter pulses red, nearly empty. My hand already reaching for the phone to finally call someone back. *(new)*

## ✗ Burnout — *lose (bad)*

> My body filed the last ticket and closed the laptop for me. Somewhere in the all-nighters and skipped meals and "I'll rest after this ship," the after-this never came. The repo is fine. I am not. The trail ends here, in bed, in the middle of a Tuesday.

**Illustration:** First-person from under the covers at noon, blinds drawn, a blade of daylight across the wall. My phone face-down and buzzing on the nightstand, a full glass of water I never drank. *(reuse: burnout)*

## ✗ Alone with the Repo — *lose (bad)*

> Everything I built still runs. The house is quiet in the wrong way. I won every argument about my evenings and lost the evenings, and the people who used to be in them. Agency you can't share with anyone, it turns out, is a nicer word for alone.

**Illustration:** My view across a dinner table set for more than one, a kid's drawing curling on the fridge behind it. My screen is the only light left on, a half-typed "sorry, swamped" frozen in a dead chat. *(new)*

## ✗ Still a Spectator — *lose (bad)*

> Twenty-six weeks went by and I watched most of them. I stayed safe, stayed employed, stayed current on everyone else's launches. The future got built by other people while I refreshed the feed and meant to start tomorrow. Tomorrow ran out.

**Illustration:** First-person, sunk into the couch in the blue wash of a feed full of other people's launch posts. Across the room my own side-project folder sits cold and unopened, three commits old. *(new)*
